About

{% markdown %} Open States is a collection of tools that make it possible for citizens to track what is happening in their state's capital by aggregating information from all fifty states, Washington, D.C., and Puerto Rico and making it available in a common format. Open States is a project of the [Sunlight Foundation](http://sunlightfoundation.com). Thank you to the [Rita Allen Foundation](http://www.ritaallenfoundation.org/) and [Minnesota Historical Society](http://mnhs.org) for their generous support. Additionally, collecting this data would not be possible without the support of a community much larger than the team here at Sunlight. A special thanks to all of our volunteer [contributors](https://github.com/sunlightlabs/openstates/graphs/contributors) and the authors and maintainers of all of the libraries that we depend upon.
Data ==== Bill, legislator, committee and event data is collected from official sources, linked at the bottom of each legislator, bill, vote, committee or event page. For more details, visit our [methodology](/methodology/) page. All Open States data is available via this website, [our API](/api/), [bulk downloads](/downloads/) and the [Open States iOS Application](http://itunes.apple.com/app/open-states/id500672932?mt=8). Notable users of Open States data include: * [National Public Radio's StateImpact](http://stateimpact.npr.org/teamblog/tag/open-states/) * [The Chicago Tribune](http://blog.apps.chicagotribune.com/2011/12/19/analyzing-illinois-pension-codes-using-the-open-states-project/) * [MinnPost.com](http://www.minnpost.com/data/2012/05/how-we-built-legislative-bill-explorer) If you're using our data and want to tell us about it, please [contact us](mailto:openstates@sunlightfoundation.com).
Code ==== All told, the website and scrapers together comprise approximately 40,000 lines of [Python](http://python.org/), all of it open source. The primary projects behind Open States: * [billy](https://github.com/sunlightlabs/billy/) - powering the backend of Open States from scraping to this very site. * [openstates](https://github.com/sunlightlabs/openstates/) - source for all of our scrapers and openstates.org templates. * [validictory](https://github.com/sunlightlabs/validictory/) - helping us keep our data in check. * [scrapelib](https://github.com/sunlightlabs/scrapelib/) - easy, powerful, error-resistant HTTP library built on [requests](https://github.com/kennethreitz/requests/).
